# Move 2: Hypothesis

**Project:** AI Bottleneck Diagnostic (Track C, 100xEngineers Cohort 7 Hackathon)
**Locked:** 20 June 2026, before any application code is written
**Track:** C — The AI Bottleneck Diagnostic

---

## The Claim

A person who uses my tool — typing out where they are stuck with AI in their
own words, then being drawn deeper through a short, mirrored conversation
rather than a standard questionnaire — will receive one single diagnostic
sentence that names their real bottleneck, and will react with genuine
recognition: either going quiet, expressing surprise that the tool named
something they had not said out loud themselves, or confirming a wall
underneath their stated complaint. They will then take a behavioral action —
either acting on the sentence, or asking unprompted "so what do I do about
this" — more often than they give a flat, polite "yeah that's kind of true"
and do nothing.

The conversational, mirroring shape of the questioning is load-bearing to
this claim. A traditional form-style Q&A is predicted to produce more
horoscope-style sentences because it does not earn the person's deeper
honesty the way a felt-heard conversation does.

---

## Result that proves me right

At least 2 out of 2 people in Move 5 show real recognition — a reaction that
goes beyond generic agreement — AND either take a concrete action on the
sentence within 24 hours, OR ask unprompted what they should do next.
Recognition counts as real when the person confirms a mechanism or behavior
they did not state themselves earlier in the conversation.

---

## Result that means I built a horoscope

Both Move 5 people give a flat or generic "yeah that's kind of true" with
no specific surprise, no unprompted follow-up question, and no stated
intent to act. The diagnostic sentence could have plausibly applied to
almost anyone stuck with AI. The conversational depth did not, in
practice, produce a sentence sharper than what a form-style Q&A would
have produced.

---

## Kill-number

If neither of my two Move 5 people show real behavioral movement — no
unprompted question, no stated intent to act, no specific recognition of
something they had not said themselves — I will write that my hypothesis
was wrong: the tool produces plausible-sounding sentences but does not
actually locate anything true enough to move a stranger. I will report
this honestly in Move 5 as a clean disproof, with the evidence and this
timestamped hypothesis as proof I did not move the goalposts.

## Move 1 signal (what I observed before locking this hypothesis)

Three by-hand interviews were conducted before this hypothesis was
written:

**Person 1 — Rhea / Maithilee.** Surface complaint: AI interfaces use
jargon and node-based metaphors she cannot map to. Sentence I delivered
live: "It's not that you don't understand APIs — none of the explanations
ever show you the actual five clicks it takes to turn that concept into
something on your screen." Live reaction: strong recognition ("Yes. Yes.
That's the problem."). Asynchronous follow-up surfaced an even sharper
mechanism in her own words: "Once the damage is done, I don't know what
to Google. Because I don't know what failed to ask for a fix."

**Person 2 — Saif.** Surface complaint: securing AI agents like
Open Interpreter is a hassle because tutorials are too complex and tools
like E2B are too expensive. Sentence I sent asynchronously: "I don't
think the issue is that you don't know how to fix the security problem.
You described the solution yourself — a Docker-style sandbox. I think
the real block is that building it yourself feels tedious and not 'real'
AI work, so you keep hoping a tutorial will appear that makes it easy
instead of building the boring version." Reaction: strong written
confirmation — "Yess, actually you are right!" — to a sentence that
contradicted his stated reason, meaning the sentence named something he
had not said out loud himself.

**Person 3 — Shefali.** Surface complaint: Claude hallucinates and loses
memory in long chats. Sentence I sent asynchronously: "You've built an
entire manual ritual — handoff summaries, timers, caveman speak — to
work around a memory limit, because the one time you tried to actually
fix the system underneath, it broke, and now you trust your own
workaround more than you trust touching the configuration again."
Reaction: response pending at time of locking this hypothesis.

Two clear hits and one pending. These three interviews are the basis for
the numbers in this hypothesis.
